# Webpage Quality Bar

A webpage is high quality only when each criterion is observable.

- Visual hierarchy: one clear primary action and scannable section priority.
- Layout: consistent grid, alignment, and section rhythm.
- Distinctiveness: a clear visual point of view, not generic AI layout filler.
- Typography: readable scale, line length, and hierarchy.
- Spacing: consistent spacing tokens with no overlap.
- Color: accessible contrast and intentional palette.
- Responsive behavior: mobile, tablet, and desktop verified.
- Accessibility: semantic landmarks, labels, focus states, alt text, contrast basics.
- Motion: purposeful and respects reduced motion.
- Interaction quality: keyboard support, visible focus, correct link/button semantics, and appropriate hit targets.
- UI states: loading, empty, dense, sparse, error, and form states are designed where relevant.
- Copywriting: specific, truthful, audience-aware, and concise.
- Conversion strategy: CTA and page flow support the goal.
- Content clarity: visitors understand what it is, who it is for, and what to do next.
- Technical correctness: build, lint, and typecheck pass.
- Performance: optimized assets and acceptable Lighthouse/bundle results.
- Maintainability: scoped components and no avoidable duplication.
- SEO basics: title, description, semantic headings, metadata, and crawlable content.
- Production readiness: human-approved UI and deployed production verification without known bugs.
